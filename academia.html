Supporting Your Academic Success

We Believe in Your Academic Success

At the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI), we are committed to supporting our students' academic success by offering a wide array of resources and services. UMSI students also have access to the extensive collections and study spaces within the University Library system, offering both digital and physical resources essential for research and coursework. Additionally, our specialized tutoring services and the UMSI Writing Center are available to support you in mastering complex subjects and enhancing your written communication skills. By utilizing these academic resources, UMSI students can thrive in their studies and achieve their educational goals.

Studying

Studying plays a crucial role in the lives of students, and the University of Michigan offers a diverse range of spaces designed to help you focus and excel in your academic pursuits. Whether you prefer the quiet solitude of a library, the collaborative energy of group study rooms, or the cozy atmosphere of campus cafes, there is a spot to suit every learning style. These thoughtfully designed environments provide the resources and ambiance needed to support productivity, helping you tackle your coursework effectively and comfortably.

Study Spaces

There are plenty of great places to study on and around campus whether your alone or with your peers. Some popular locations include:

  • Hatcher Graduate Library
  • Michigan Union
  • Pierpoint Commons

Want to plan ahead? Here are some places where you can reserve your spot so you don't have to worry about finding the perfect spot to do your work. Students are typically able to book these spaces 7 days in advance and can be reserved for up to 4 hours.

  • Shapiro Undergraduate Library Study Rooms
  • Hatcher Graduate Library 3rd Floor Study Carrels
  • North Quad (NQ) Classrooms

Tutoring

Tutoring at the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI) is a collaborative and supportive resource designed to help students excel in their academic pursuits. Tutors are often knowledgeable peers or graduate students with expertise in core areas such as programming, user experience research, data analysis, and information management.Sessions are tailored to individual or group needs, providing personalized guidance on coursework, projects, or concepts students find challenging.


Beyond academic support, tutors foster confidence, encouraging students to tackle complex problems independently. With flexible scheduling and a commitment to inclusivity, UMSI tutoring empowers students to thrive in their rigorous, interdisciplinary program.

Sweetland Writing Center

Sweetland offers one-to-one, in-person and online writing assistance for U-M Ann Arbor graduate students in Writing Workshop. This service allows you to meet individually with an experienced faculty consultant at any stage of writing, from getting started to final revisions. You can get help with understanding assignments, generating ideas, developing arguments, organizing and structuring your project, using evidence and sources, and clarifying your expression.

Sweetland Writing Center can assist graduate students with:

  • Course Assignments
  • Dissertation Chapters
  • Conference Papers
  • Articles for publications
  • Research Proposals
  • Personal/research/teaching/DEI Statements (2 max)

For more information, visit the Sweetland Writing Center website

UMSI Peer Tutoring

In partnership with our UMSI faculty, the UMSI Peer Tutoring program is an opportunity for UMSI students and students taking UMSI courses to engage with peer tutors and receive assistance and support in UMSI courses. The core course areas that UMSI peer tutoring targets is programming and math related classes, the availability areas are listed below.


UMSI Programming Peer Tutoring Course Areas

  1. SI 506 Programming I
  2. SI 507 Intermediate Programming
  3. General Python Support
  4. SIADS 505 Data Manipulation

UMSI MADS Math Peer Tutoring Subject Areas

  1. Linear Algebra
  2. Statistics
  3. General Math related course support
  4. SIADS 502 Math Methods I

To see if peer tutoring is right for you, visit the UMSI Tutoring website

Academic Support Services

Services for Students with Disabilities office (SSD)

Embedded in the strategic plan of the University of Michigan is a commitment to ensure equal opportunity for all individuals. Services for Students with Disabilities (SSD) recognizes disability as an integral part of diversity and is committed to creating an inclusive and equitable educational environment for disabled students. SSD is a partner to students, faculty, and staff in the pursuit to develop leaders and citizens who will challenge the present and enrich the future.


To learn more and get connected, visit the SSD website

English Language Institute (ELI)

The ELI offers Academic English courses and workshops for international graduate students and other speakers of English as an additional language. The ELI also offers Graduate Language Clinics which provide international graduate students, GSIs, scholars, faculty, and staff with one-on-one language and academic support. Clinics are staffed by ELI lecturers or by GSIs working under close supervision of experienced faculty.


For more information, visit the English Language Institute website
