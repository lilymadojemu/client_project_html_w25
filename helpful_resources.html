We're Here to Help

While attending the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI), students may encounter unexpected situations that lead them to need external help to meet their needs. UMSI aims to create a space that can address these issues and not have them impede students' ability or potential to succeed in their acdemics.

Food

Services to help students maintain healthy habits, find access to food, and seek assistance when struggling to buy food.

Maize and Blue Cupboard

College students and staff are experiencing food insecurity at alarming rates. The Maize and Blue Cupboard is here to provide an immediate and comprehensive response for the U-M community. By offering resources, educational opportunities, compassionate support and more, we help students develop the skills to make informed decisions.

Resources Provided:

  • Food: Produce, dairy, meat, bread, frozen and shelf-stable foods (i.e. rice, cans, tuna, etc.)
  • Kitchen & Cooking: Dishes, silverware, pots & pans, Tupperware, cutting boards, knives, and other kitchenware
  • Personal & Household:Trash bags, toilet paper, cleaning supplies, hygiene products, school supplies, baby items and more

Michigan Dining Nutrition Services

Michigan Dining offers various nutrition services and resources. The online MyNutrition tool helps to identify common allergens and nutritional values for on-campus foods. Students with meal plans are eligible for free one-on-one nutrition counseling.

Financial

Financial resources to help with immediate and long-term needs, such as emergencies, college funding and employment.
