Life Outside of the Classroom

Enjoy Your Student Life

At the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI), we recognize that achieving a healthy balance between academics and personal well-being is essential for overall success and fulfillment. That's why we offer a wide range of opportunities and resources to help students make the most of their time outside the classroom. From student organizations and campus events to wellness programs and recreational activities, UMSI provides countless ways to relax, connect, and recharge. This page highlights the many avenues available for enriching your personal life while fostering a strong sense of community during your time at UMSI.

Professional & Personal Development

The University of Michigan offers a wealth of opportunities to support both personal growth and professional development. From engaging workshops and networking events to counseling and advising, there’s something for everyone to explore. Here’s a snapshot of some of the exciting opportunities available to help you thrive on campus.

Workshops

Workshops provide an excellent opportunity to expand your knowledge and develop new skills. Here are some of the upcoming workshops available on campus to help you grow personally and professionally.

  • Financial Empowerment Series
  • Food Literacy for All Series
  • Writing with ChatGPT

Career & Education Counseling

CEW+ provides professionally trained and experienced counselors work within a developmental framework to help individuals consider their options, make informed choices, and both define and resolve problems, while focusing on issues of achieving their career goals and balance between school, work, and family.

Typical counseling services for students may include discussions around:


  • Navigating the University experience
  • Tailored support
  • Work/Life balance issues
  • Financial aid options
  • Emergency funding

For more information, visit the CEW+ website

Build Community

Graduate school can be challenging and, at times, isolating, but building connections with others can make a significant difference. Taking the time to foster relationships, whether through student organization or campus events, can provide valuable support, encouragement, and a sense of belonging throughout your academic journey.

Events

Attending events around campus is a fantastic way to meet new people and build lasting friendships. Whether it's through academic seminars, social gatherings, or cultural celebrations, these events provide an opportunity to connect with others and engage in meaningful experiences. Some of the events UM has had include:


  • First-Generation Fall Community Dinner: The First-Gen Community Dinner is an opportunity for first-generation college students to connect with each other and learn more about the First-Gen Gateway.
  • QTBIPOC Kickbacks: Monthly gatherings that open up the wall between MESA and Spectrum Center for community and meals, centering students who are queer and trans Black, indigenous, and people of color.
  • Winterfest: An annual event held at the University of Michigan that celebrates the winter season with a variety of fun and festive activities.

This list only scratches the surface of the types of events offerered at the University of Michigan. Events are always happening so be on the lookout and don't be afraid to try something new!
